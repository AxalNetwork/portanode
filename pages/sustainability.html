--- layout: page title: Sustainability permalink: /sustainability/ eyebrow: Company subtitle: How AXAL thinks about embodied carbon, redeployment, and the long lifecycle of a container-scale building. --- ## The premise Most "sustainable infrastructure" arguments are about operations — kilowatt-hours, water draw, recycled inputs. We care about all of that, but the bigger lever for a deployable system is **the second life of every module**. A traditional field building gets demolished at end-of-project. An AXAL module gets craned onto a flat-rack and shipped to its next deployment. ## The numbers we publish - **Embodied carbon:** ~38 t CO₂e per module (factory-gate, third-party-verified, EN 15804). - **Operating carbon:** Volt + grid-forming firmware lets most stacks run net-positive on irradiance >4 kWh/m²/day. - **Redeployment rate:** 64% of modules sold before 2024 have moved at least once. - **End-of-life:** every module has a published disassembly plan; recyclable mass is 91% by weight. ## What we don't claim - We don't sell "carbon-neutral" stacks. Carbon offsets are a separate conversation. - We don't claim the supply chain is perfect. The lithium in our LFP cells is sourced under [our supplier code](/legal/privacy/), but it's still mined. - We don't recommend AXAL for crypto mining at grid-mix sites without curtailment-additionality. See [Crypto mining](/use-cases/crypto-mining/). ## Reporting Customers running compliance disclosures (CSRD, SEC climate, TCFD) get a per-deployment lifecycle data export from axalOS. Talk to your account engineer for the format.
