The "edge compute" conversation in 2026 is finally past the marketing phase. The customers signing real contracts care about three things: cost-per-kilowatt-IT, time-to-energise, and the SLA on the link.

## Where the cost actually is

For a typical 120 kW edge pod, hardware is ~55% of total deployed cost. The rest is the building, the cooling, the power conditioning, and the connectivity. Most of that "rest" is what container-scale providers like AXAL collapse to a single SKU.

## Time-to-energise is the disruption

A traditional edge build is 9–14 months. A configured-and-shipped AXAL Edge Compute stack is 8 weeks from order to commissioning. That delta is what wins the deal — not the per-watt cost.

## Where this goes

We expect three patterns to dominate in 2027:

1. **Tower retrofit** at 5G sites, mostly Volt-driven.
2. **Regional cloud nodes** in markets where data residency matters and latency dominates.
3. **On-site ML inference** at large industrial sites, replacing today's "ship the data to a hyperscaler" model.

AXAL is built for all three. The interesting fight is between us and a handful of competitors who are making the same bet on container scale.
