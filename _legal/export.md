AXAL hardware and software are subject to applicable export-control laws, including U.S. Export Administration Regulations (EAR), EU Regulation 2021/821, and UK Strategic Export Control Lists.

## 1. End-use restrictions

AXAL will not knowingly supply Products to:

- Parties on the EU Consolidated, U.S. OFAC SDN, BIS Entity, or UK Sanctions lists.
- End-uses involving the development, production, or use of weapons of mass destruction or unauthorised military end-uses.
- Comprehensively sanctioned jurisdictions, currently including (but not limited to) Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, the Crimea / DNR / LNR regions, and other regions as designated.

## 2. Customer obligations

By placing an order, the Customer represents that the Products will not be re-exported, transferred, or used in violation of applicable export controls. The Customer agrees to provide reasonable end-use documentation on request and to flow these obligations to its successors and sub-purchasers.

## 3. Encryption & dual-use software

axalOS includes cryptographic functionality. The current EAR classification of axalOS is published in the spec book and is updated when we change export-relevant components. EU dual-use classifications are referenced in our quote documentation.

## 4. Defense engagements

Defense customers and prime contractors are evaluated under additional procedures, including end-user verification, sovereign-customer review, and any required licences. See [Defense use cases](/use-cases/defense/) and contact <span class="font-mono">compliance@axal.dev</span> before submitting requirements.

## 5. Reporting concerns

If you believe an AXAL Product may be at risk of export-control violation, contact <span class="font-mono">compliance@axal.dev</span>. Reports are reviewed by AXAL's compliance officer; whistle-blower protection applies under Dutch and EU law.
